Joseph Kuhn's Education

Stony Lane Elementary

My favorite class in Stony Lane was the computers class, because this was when I first started using and learning about computers. It's where I learned how to type, as well as how to navigate the internet and other applications. We also got to play games occasionally, which made the class even more fun for a young version of myself.

A fun memory of Stony Lane was the big 4th grade sleepover that we had. They planned a bunch of fun activities for us, and at the end of them all, we slept in the school gym in sleeping bags while our teachers and other volunteers made sure we were safe. It was an unforgettable experience.

West Brook Middle School

My favorite class in West Brook was the cooking class that I took in 5th grade. Up until that point, I had never cooked anything before, so it was a brand new experience for me that I really enjoyed. I learned how to make simple foods like eggs, and evolve those skills into more detailed meals like "egg in a frame." We also made some desserts, such as ice pops.

A fun memory of West Brook was our trip to Washington D.C. In the 8th grade, our entire grade took a bus down to D.C. for a few days to explore the area and gain a better appreciation for our country. We slept in hotel rooms, and had a mini dinner/dance in one of the hotel's bigger rooms on the first night.

Paramus High School

My favorite class in Paramus High School was AP Computer Science. This was the first time I ever coded in my life, so it was my introduction to something that I'm very passionate about and something that I want to do for my career. I learned the basic concepts of computer science and learned how to use Java. We made our own personal project at the end of the year after we took the AP exam.

A fun memory of Paramus High School was our pool day. It was at the end of senior year, and all of the seniors were excused from classes for the day. We all went to the local town pool where we got to spend the day swimming in the various pools, playing volleyball on the court, or even just relaxing on the grass. My friends and I ordered Dominos for lunch.

Stevens Institute of Technology

My favorite class at Stevens Institute of Technology so far was CPE 360, Data Structures and Computer Algorithms. This was the first time I took the next step out of basic computer programming to learn about different types of data structures and their functions. For example, I learned about stacks, queues, and linked lists, as well as binary search trees, and I started to understand how a lot of different websites and applications are set up as efficiently as they are.

A fun memory of Stevens was when I first joined Kappa Sigma. It was a brand new experience for me, especially since I didn't expect to join Greek Life when I came to Stevens as a freshman. I got to truly understand what fraternities were about and what the values that Kappa Sigma held were. I felt extremely welcomed by everyone in the fraternity, and I found my home away from home, where I could be part of a second (and much larger) family while I wasn't with my real one.
